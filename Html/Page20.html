The sun was slowly setting, and everybody was worn out. Apollo was still working on his notes, Dusty was about to take a nap on their floating couch. Alp was looking at his dinosaur like he was expecting it to move. Aegis blew out his candle that smelled like vanilla--which everyone was confused on how it was a magical item-- and walked over to Apollo and Tarda.

Aegis: Hey, how does this magic know what everything is?

Apollo: Huh?

Aegis: I mean like Atlas put in the prompt: cool, and it gave him sunglasses that matched his own but sizzles with lightning when he makes a sharp head turn. How did it know what cool meant.

Apollo: Yeah there is an aspect of subjectivity that we haven't figured out yet. I still haven't gotten to the philosophical analysis, because I was too busy on the practical analysis. Tarda has been looking over the code to see if anything else is there.

Coy was sitting on one of the chairs looking at her watch. She looked up and gave a suggestion.

Coy: If it is subjective, if we put in something like "The best thing ever" would it give the best thing ever for us?

Tarda looked up from his laptop with an intrigued expression.

Tarda: You know that actually sounds interesting. Let's give it a go.

By this time everybody was listening in on that conversation because it broke the silence. Everybody tiringly gathered around the table to see what would happen. Tarda typed in.

PROMPT:THE BEST THING EVER

Eri: I would say we all press the buttons at the same time. So it's like the best thing ever for all of us right?

Everyone looked around and nodded in agreement. Dusty started off the countdown.

3!

2!

1!

The room bathed in dark green light.
